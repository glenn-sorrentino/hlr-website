Meet Micah Lee, a fearless journalist, innovative programmer, and staunch advocate for digital rights. With his unique blend of technical expertise and investigative journalism, Micah has made it his mission to empower readers and uncover the truth, all while defending privacy in our increasingly interconnected world. Now, you have the chance to explore the mind behind some of the most groundbreaking exposés and revelations of our time with his latest book, "Hacks, Leaks, and Revelations."

As the Director of Information Security at The Intercept, Micah has a track record of transforming complex technical concepts into easily digestible information. His work on digital and operational security, encryption tools, whistleblowing, and hacking has garnered widespread praise from experts in the field. Notable figures like Eva Galperin, Director of Cybersecurity at the Electronic Frontier Foundation, and Gabriella Coleman, Harvard Professor and Founder of Hack_Curio, have lauded Micah's book as a fantastic and friendly introduction for anyone interested in analyzing large data sets.

Micah is not just an expert in the field; he's an innovator. His contributions to open-source software and privacy tools, such as OnionShare, Dangerzone, and Semiphemeral, reflect his commitment to empowering users with the knowledge and tools they need to protect their privacy.

His illustrious career has seen him work as a staff technologist at the Electronic Frontier Foundation, co-founder of the Freedom of the Press Foundation, and a Tor Project core contributor. Micah has also served as an advisor at Distributed Denial of Secrets.

In "Hacks, Leaks, and Revelations," Micah breaks down the complexities of handling data leaks, arming journalists and researchers with the tools necessary to mine, analyze, and make sense of the digital gold mines that surround us. This invaluable guide has earned rave reviews from fellow journalists, researchers, and cybersecurity experts, all of whom agree that Micah's book will equip you with the knowledge and tools you need to navigate the world of leaked datasets.
